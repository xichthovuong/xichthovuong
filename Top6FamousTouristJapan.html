  • 1) Himeji Castle



    Himeji Castle in Himeji, Japan, is a complex representing the modern period’s architecture of Japan. Himeji was built in 1346 on the peak of the Himeyama hill, which is 45,6 meter higher than the basic sea level. It is one of the intact complexes throughout a history of a lot of intense earthquakes and wars. This castle’s building material is 100% wood instead of stones. Its defense system is sophisticated like a labyrinth, and visitors cannot find the way out without the tour guide’s instruction. In 1993, Himeji is acknowledged by UNESCO as a World Cultural Heritage.


  • 2) Golden Pavilion



    Located in Kyoto, Golden Pavilion – also known as Kinkakuji – is surrounded by a large lake, mountains, and trees. The Pavilion has three main floors with different design and decoration. The first floor was built based on Shinden style with its pillars made by natural wood. The two other floors’ design style is Bukkle and a little Chinese, both decorated by gold, making them really impressive and palatial. Supervising the Golden Pavilion are two buddha statues Shaka and Yoshimitsu and a bodhisattva statue named Kannon.


  • 3) Yakushima Island



    Yakushima Island is located in Kagoshima Prefecture of Japan with exotic pentagons. The highest point of the island is Miyanoura - dake with a height of 1.935m with a rich animal carpet has been recognized by UNESCO as a natural heritage of the world.


  • 4) Mount Fuji



    Mount Fuji is a volcanic volcano that is still active and also the highest mountain in Japan. This is one of the famous symbols of the sunrise country. Mt. Fuji is 3,776m high and covered with snow all year round. Mount Fuji is recognized as a world cultural heritage site in June 2013. It is a scenic spot, one of the most beautiful tourist destinations in Japan.

  • 6) Horyuji Temple



    Horyuji Temple is a Buddhist temple in Nara Prefecture - Japan. The temple was built in 607 and is the oldest wooden architecture in the world. Horyuji was recognized as a World Heritage Site in 1993 and is also recognized by the Government of Japan as National Security.
